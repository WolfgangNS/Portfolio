About Me

In my senior year of high school, my class watched the film Gattaca, which explores ethics, self-determinism, and nature vs. nurture in the era of whole-genome sequencing. Then realizing that Hg39 was accessible to everyone, I wanted to jumpstart my career in computational biology while there was still a gold rush.
I am a self-taught, driven graduate of biochemistry (B.Sc.), passionate in discovering efficient computational workflows that reduce overhead in the wet-lab. I love to make software free, accessible across platforms, and able to load quickly and efficiently. I believe that having an abudnantly clear vision, assisted by in silico tools, can reduce overhead costs and needless trial-and-error in the lab.

GTF-to-matrix

OpenSNP circular dendrogram

This project seeks to create a family tree diagram of a broad range of individuals who volunteered their genetic data to OpenSNP. This involved seeking out an R library for plotting a circular tree diagram, researching methods to calculate “relatedness” based on SNP data, and downloading and integrating an enormous dataset from Archive.org.
